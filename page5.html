Chapter 2

--The Betrayal--





The Real Monster

Our mothers tell us that
there are no monsters
under our beds,
or hidden inside our closets
but they don't warn us
that sometimes monsters
come dressed as people
that claim to love you
more than the sun
loves the moon.

--Nikita Gil--

As time passes, they change, their touch now feeling cold;
their words now empty;
their eyes melancholic with remorse.
You question once again,
confrontation leading to constant bickering and excuses.
